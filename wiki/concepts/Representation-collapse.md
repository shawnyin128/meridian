# Representation collapse

## What It Is

`Representation collapse` is a compiled preliminary-knowledge concept: a recurring mechanism or implementation background that appears across papers/methods and affects how a researcher understands, implements, debugs, or ablates the method family.

## Why It Matters

- Self-supervised or predictive representation systems need checks that embeddings retain useful variation rather than collapsing to trivial or shortcut solutions.

## Implementation Implications

- Track embedding variance, nearest-neighbor diversity, and downstream transfer alongside training loss.
- Use fixed evaluation probes when changing representation objectives.

## Common Failure Modes

- Loss decreases because the representation collapses or exploits a shortcut.
- A probe improves on one downstream task while losing coverage of rare factors.

## Minimal Checks / Probes

- Measure embedding covariance rank or variance over a held-out batch.
- Run linear-probe or nearest-neighbor sanity checks with frozen encoders.
